No Pressure

Age when created: 17
Medium: Photoshop
Tools used: Stock photography, chalk brushes, Wacom Intuos 4
Personal satisfaction: 4/5

This was the graphic I made when applying to Media Arts + Practice, which is meant to represent my personal struggles in transitioning to adulthood. This was born in a time of stress and uncertainty in my life. College was fast approaching, which meant finishing high school with good grades, meeting application deadlines, graduating, moving away from family. This wasn’t just a part of my application. It was an outlet for all of the pressure put on me by school, family, society, and myself.

Looking back at this graphic now, although I’d make some cosmetic changes (like using an actual photo of myself instead of stock photos), it still hits really hard. Though it’s a good reminder of my ability to use my craft as my diary and my therapist.
