Shark in Cup

Age when created: 17
Medium: Charcoal
Tools used: Charcoal, poster paper
Personal satisfaction: 2/5

I drew this shark in a cup in my art class senior year of high school. It was meant as an exercise in noticing the distortion water causes, and in using charcoal. I was pretty happy with it at the time, though I think the only reason this was even in my portfolio for college apps was that it was like proof that "hey, I draw studies from life!" It’s really the epitome of going through the motions - drawing it because of a class, and submitting it because that’s what the art schools want to see. If I could go back and tell my younger self to put things with more emotional significance to me in my portfolio, I would.
